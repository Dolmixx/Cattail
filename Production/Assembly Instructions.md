# Assembly Instructions 
Video Tutorial Soon after building begins.

1. The base 

The cattail has a “base” which is its face as pictured below:

<img width="1920" height="1080" alt="Cattail Face" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/27d3c841-eb69-45de-955d-f2b82a648ac4" />

There should be 4 printed out leaves that can be easily inserted at an angle into the slots on the bottom. 

<img width="1920" height="1080" alt="cattail leaves" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/cb607faa-3e22-4621-bcfa-c3ea8dbfd27f" />

They have a section at the end that “hooks” onto an internal flap so that the leaves cannot be pulled out easily. Each leaf can be put into whichever leaf holder, make sure that the leaves are facing the right direction. To check this, make sure the leaf is curved downwards instead of upwards. To remove the leaves, perform the same action but in reverse. 

<img width="1920" height="1080" alt="cattail w leaves" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/00050893-7fdd-4744-8a07-fe5cbecfc854" />

The leaves have no functional purpose other than aesthetics and can be disregarded if you so choose. 

2. The head

The head of the cattail follows a similar design. There is a hat and two ears. The hat should sit on top of the face, making sure to align the slope properly. There is a 5 degree angle on the inside where both pieces land, this should make it so the rim of the front of the hat sits above the Cattails eyebrows. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="cattail hat" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ee2abebc-722e-4508-b28b-7f666a34df8f" />

<img width="800" height="600" alt="cattail w hat and stff" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/0fcc0647-8f4d-4e4c-be5b-f04482df9336" />

The ears are similar to the leaves where you can simply place them into the dedicated slots on top. These serve no functionality other than appearance and can be discarded if your intention is solely to have a working turret. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="cat" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e7d01421-4420-42a7-b18b-ddef340e565b" />

3. The tail (Not to be confused with the main cattail portion)

There are three parts to the tail, the lower, middle, and upper portion. They each have a different purpose. 

Start by taking the lower portion of the tail and inserting it into the back of the Cattail face. There will be a slot in between the back leaves that is raised higher, that is meant for the tail. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="cat back" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6844ccc6-5d9d-4b61-a26c-029329568755" />

<img width="800" height="600" alt="cat back 2" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/68da2f98-deeb-4d44-ae63-2fea683c81d6" />

You will have noticed by now it also features a hole that leads straight into the face, that is on purpose for wires to fit through later. (More about that in the electronic assembly instructions)

Before attaching the middle portion of the tail, it is suggested to screw in the servo motor. If you wish not to have electronics and simply wish to have a model of the Cattail feel free to disregard this step. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="servo" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6ea39c6b-4a7c-4b89-be2b-9d73c85ecf8d" />

As you assemble you may find it convenient to also assemble the electronics simultaneously. That is up to you to decide. 

Ensure that the middle tail lines up with the lower portion. This can be done by making sure it is sloping down towards the Cat face as well as looking to see if the servo circle opening is facing the back versus being closer to the front. 

The upper portion of the tail can be easily attached by lining up the servo motor opening to the opening on the bottom of the upper portion of the tail. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="cat upper tail" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/110b8ac0-5f44-4900-88f6-6e9b05ceb174" />

4. The shooter mechanism (The brown portion of the tail)
Note: If you do not wish to have electronics and solely want a Cattail statue, disregard this step and simply attach the cylinder portion approximately .5cm into the tail. 

The last step is to assemble the shooting mechanism. There should be 4 pieces total, the first piece shaped like an L will be referred to as the joint. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="joint" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2157d3fb-f0ea-489b-8728-41857862c5bf" />

The second piece is a small object similar to a T shape with a long arm on one side will be referred to as the T shape. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="t shape" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/5093acac-eb54-4dbd-8770-2b7680911d2a" />

The third piece is an object resembling a box with a hole at its side which will be referred to as the base. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="base" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ef3bcf8f-7e9a-4258-b2c4-3c10ebec287b" />

Finally the fourth piece resembling a cylinder will be referred to as the corndog. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="hotdog" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/11daebf2-d373-4b35-b0a7-3cfe15d0bc70" />

Start by placing a DC motor in the base. There is a hole that should fit it, and push it fully in. You will know it is properly secured when the motor rod is sticking out the other side, as well as there is no more space for you to continue pushing. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="motor + base 1" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e557953f-ecbd-4574-b71f-5ed59a87273e" />
<img width="800" height="600" alt="motor + base 2" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/7ebfaee3-f872-4376-b968-64e53b6c3b27" />

Next attach the joint (the L piece) to the motor by looking at the side that has a hole in it and putting it through. Make sure it is securely attached by pushing it all the way through. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="l + base + motor" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f5583e5e-fcb8-49ce-ab26-c3171af16606" />

Place the T shape part on top of the base. There should be a 2mm section that the T shape can slide into. The arm of the T shape should be facing the right side, being able to be touched by the joint. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="ever" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/9755fcf2-c992-4e98-bc1f-cf36125d249a" />

You will see an identical looking T shape on the front of the base, attached to a small rubber band that connects the T shapes of both. The idea is that the joint will push the T part away and the rubber band will snap it back forward. 

Take the corndog (cylinder piece) and you will notice theres a hole on one end, and on the other end there will be something that looks like two eyes. On the side with the two eyes, attach the sonar sensor. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="sensor hotdog" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e2232f81-04c2-47d6-a8a2-38e3078b646e" />

The sensor should be placed in a position in which it sticks out in front. There are 4 screw holes to fasten it in. 

Next you will notice on the “floor” of the hotdog on the same side as the sensor there is a rail way like structure. Pointing the base (with all completed steps previously) in where the tiny 2mm hole is facing outwards, you can “snap” it into place by lining it up to the track. 

<img width="800" height="600" alt="everythin" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e2b3771b-792e-4542-907c-be3fd7862a3e" />

Then attach the whole thing to the upper tail, inserting it approx. .5 cm. 

<img width="1280" height="720" alt="Cattail Full" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f903bc39-0b4f-4414-ac8c-23c4878dacc9" />

Congratulations you now have your very own Cattail! 

# Electronic Assembly Instructions 
*images will be added when building begins*
1. Servo motor

Wire as shown in the [wiring diagram](https://github.com/Dolmixx/Cattail/blob/71b2e4feb15a2f312b79d93c6bd7dd4ea0bebad7/Wiring%20Diagram.pdf). The tail is hollow, feed the wires through the tail, use additional jumper wires if necessary. 

2. DC Motor + Sonar Sensor

Both should be connected as shown in the wiring diagram. Ensure that the dc motor has its own power supply. 

Feed through the wires through the tail on either side of the gap, make sure the servo rotation does not interfere with the connection.
